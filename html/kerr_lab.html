Why Kerr Black Holes are never "Perfect"

In General Relativity, a Kerr black hole is a mathematical ideal. However, once we introduce the $\sigma_P$ limit, the horizon becomes slightly "smeared". This results in a small but calculated departure from a perfect thermal black-body spectrum. This coefficient $c_0$ represents the intrinsic non-thermality required by the quantization of spacetime.
