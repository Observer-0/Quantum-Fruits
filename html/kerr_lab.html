Who is Roy Kerr?

In 1963, Roy Kerr discovered the mathematical 4-vector for a rotating mass. Before Kerr, we only understood stationary (Schwarzschild) black holes. Since every object in the universe rotates, Kerr's solution is the "real" description of cosmic singularities.

Who is Saul Teukolsky?

In the 1970s, Saul Teukolsky derived the equations that describe how a rotating black hole vibrates. His "Teukolsky Master Equation" allows us to calculate how gravitational and electromagnetic waves (information) interact with the horizon.

The Challenge: Mathematical Ideals

In General Relativity, a Kerr black hole is a "perfect" mathematical construct. Its horizon is infinitely sharp, and it absorbs everything without leaving a trace—leading directly to the Information Paradox.

However, an infinitely sharp horizon requires infinite energy to probe. In nature, no boundary is truly infinite; there is always a scale where the "smearing" of quantum effects becomes relevant.

The Σₚ Resolution

The Zander model replaces the ideal horizon with the Teukolsky Smearing Limit.

  • Horizon Smearing ($c_0$): Quantization ($ \sigma_P $) creates a fundamental "blur" at the horizon.
  • Non-Thermal Signature: This blur ensures the Hawking radiation is not purely random (thermal) but carries the "echoes" of the interior action.
  • Physical Realism: By removing the "infinite sharpness", the $c_0$ coefficient provides the mechanism for information to escape, making the black hole a physical, unitary object.
